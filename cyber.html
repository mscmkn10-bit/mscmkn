Stay Safe Online 🔐

The internet is useful, but it also comes with risks. These simple tips will help students stay safe from common cyber threats.

Password Safety

Always use strong passwords that are hard to guess. Avoid using your name, date of birth, or simple passwords like "123456". Try to use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Never use the same password on multiple websites.

Phishing Awareness

Be careful with emails, messages, or links that ask for your personal information. Hackers often create fake websites that look real. Always check the website URL before logging in and never click suspicious links.

Safe Browsing

Only download apps or files from trusted sources. Avoid using cracked or pirated software, as they may contain malware. Keep your browser and system updated to stay protected from new threats.

Social Media Privacy

Do not share personal information like your address, phone number, or school details publicly. Keep your social media accounts private and avoid accepting requests from unknown people.

OTP & Scam Awareness

Never share your OTP with anyone, even if they claim to be from a bank or company. Many scams involve fake calls, messages, or job offers. Always verify before trusting such requests.
